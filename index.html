Figure idea adapted from G.H. Hardy, Pure Mathematics, Cambridge University Press, 1952

Let {an} be a sequence of real numbers, and let L (the red line) be a real number. The sequence {an} converges to L (denoted limn→∞ an = L) if for every ε > 0, there exists a positive integer N (green line) such that for all n ≥ N, |an − L| < ε.     Stewart, James. Calculus: Early Transcendentals (8th ed.), Brooks/Cole, 2015.
